On May 31, 2025, the Allianz Arena in Munich hosted the UEFA Champions League Final between Paris Saint-Germain (PSG) and Inter Milan.
This marked PSG's second appearance in a Champions League final, while Inter Milan returned to the final for the second time in three years. The match was a showcase of contrasting football philosophies and club ownership models: PSG, backed by Qatari investment, featured a youthful and dynamic squad under manager Luis Enrique, whereas Inter, owned by American firm Oaktree Capital, relied on experienced players and tactical discipline under Simone Inzaghi. The game concluded with PSG securing a 2-1 victory over Inter Milan, earning their first-ever Champions League title and completing a historic treble for the club. The Allianz Arena, with a reduced capacity of 64,500 for the final, provided a vibrant atmosphere for this memorable encounter.

inter milan

nter Milan, officially known as Football Club Internazionale Milano, is one of Italy’s most successful and historic football clubs. Founded in 1908, the club is based in Milan, Lombardy, and shares the iconic San Siro Stadium with city rivals AC Milan.
Known by fans as "Nerazzurri" (black and blues), Inter has a rich tradition of domestic and international success, including 20 Serie A titles, 9 Coppa Italia trophies, and 3 UEFA Champions League titles (in 1964, 1965, and 2010). The club made headlines in 2010 by winning a historic treble—Serie A, Coppa Italia, and Champions League—under coach José Mourinho. Inter is known for its strong defense, tactical discipline, and passionate global fanbase. In recent years, under the guidance of coach Simone Inzaghi, Inter has reestablished itself as a powerhouse in both Italian and European football.

The Allianz Arena is a world-famous football stadium located in Munich, Germany. It is the home ground of FC Bayern Munich, one of Europe’s top football clubs.
Opened in 2005, the stadium is known for its unique and futuristic design, especially its glowing exterior panels, which can change colors depending on the team playing. The Allianz Arena has a seating capacity of around 75,000 and regularly hosts Bundesliga, Champions League, and international matches. It was one of the stadiums used during the 2006 FIFA World Cup and is also set to host matches for UEFA Euro 2024.
